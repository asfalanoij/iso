# ISO 37001:2025 Anti-Bribery Management System (ABMS)
## Delta Analysis and Implementation Strategy

### 1. Executive Summary
ISO 37001 has been revised in 2025 to increase its effectiveness, integrate better with the latest Harmonized Structure (HS), and strengthen the emphasis on governance and sustainable anti-bribery cultures. As Telin is planning for new certification or transition, understanding these deltas is critical to achieving a successful Stage 1 and Stage 2 CB Audit by October 2026.

### 2. Key Deltas (2016 vs. 2025 version)

| 2016 Clause | 2025 Clause | Key Changes & Additions | Strategic Impact for Telin |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **N/A** | **4.1 Understanding Org (Climate Change)** | Requires evaluating if climate change factors impact bribery risks (e.g., green supply chain disruption causing corrupt procurement). | Must document a climate change statement in the ABMS context/scope. |
| **5.1.2** | **5.1.2 Governing Body** | Expanded requirements for the Governing Body's oversight. | Telin's Board/Commissioners must formally review ABMS performance. |
| **N/A** | **5.1.3 Anti-Bribery Culture** | Formalized requirement for top management to build, maintain, and promote an ethical culture. | HR/Internal Comm campaigns needed. Must demonstrate culture measurement (e.g., employee surveys). |
| **5.3.2** | **5.3.3 Anti-Bribery Function** | Renamed from "Anti-bribery compliance function". Stronger emphasis on independence and direct access to Top Management. | Formal appointment letter required. Must show unhindered access to Board of Directors (BoD) bypassing regular reporting lines. |
| **8.7** | **8.8 Gifts, Hospitality, Donations** | Expanded detail on handling political contributions, sponsorships, and community benefits. | Revise Gift Register. Ensure sponsorships (e.g., industry events) are screened through ABMS. |
| **N/A** | **8.7 Conflicts of Interest** | New dedicated clause. Explicit requirements to identify, declare, evaluate, and manage CoI. | Requires a formal, digital CoI declaration process across all high-risk roles globally (ID, SG, HK, AU, US). |

### 3. Implementation Strategy for Telin (2026 Certification Journey)

#### Phase 1: Gap Assessment & Structure Update (Weeks 0-8)
1. **Scope Extension:** Ensure the ABMS scope formally encompasses Telin USA (and addresses FCPA risk), Telin HK (ICAC), and Telin AU.
2. **Context Document Revamp:** Update Context of Organization (Clause 4.1, 4.2) to specifically address Climate Change impacts and modernized Interested Parties mapping.
3. **Appoint Anti-Bribery Function:** Officially designate the "Anti-Bribery Function" (formerly ABCF) with documented, direct reporting access to the Board of Directors.

#### Phase 2: Operations & Policy Enhancement (Weeks 8-16)
1. **Conflict of Interest (CoI) Drive:** Launch an automated, mandatory CoI declaration for all management, sales, and procurement staff.
2. **Enhanced Due Diligence (Clause 8.2):** Integrate automated ABMS Due Diligence into Telin's ERP/Procurement pipeline for all high-risk vendors and M&A targets.
3. **Culture Program (Clause 5.1.3):** Execute a top-down anti-bribery culture campaign led by the CEO, documenting "Tone from the Top" communications.

#### Phase 3: Readiness & Internal Audit (Weeks 17-24)
1. **Internal Audit (Clause 9.2):** Conduct an internal audit using external consultants to ensure unbiased assessment against the 2025 standard.
2. **Management Review (Clause 9.3):** Execute a dual-review process (Top Management review followed by Governing Body oversight).

#### Phase 4: Certification Body (CB) Audit (Weeks 25-30)
1. **Stage 1 Audit (October):** Focus heavily on the design of the newly revised standard (Culture, CoI, Reporting Lines, Context).
2. **Stage 2 Audit (Late October):** Focus on operational compliance, checking due diligence records and CoI declarations, especially across international subsidiaries.
